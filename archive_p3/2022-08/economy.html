Spanish stores banned from setting air-conditioners below 27ºC to save energy
Spanish offices, stores and hospitality venues will no longer be allowed to set their cooling systems below 27º Celsius (81º Fahrenheit) in summer nor raise heating above 19ºC in the winter under a new set of energy-saving measures passed Monday. Shops will also have to keep doors closed and heating systems must be checked more often, Spanish Ecological Transition Minister Teresa Ribera said. The measures include switching off store window lights after 10 p.m. Street lighting will not be affected. The emergency measures were announced following high summer temperatures and gas shortages due to sanctions against Russia, which have put a strain on power systems across Europe. Earlier last week, Spanish Prime Minister Pedro Sanchez proposed ministers, public officials and private sector employees not wear neckties during hot summer months "so they can also contribute to saving electricity."
