How US would avoid default
Earlier in 2023, the US started talking about a possible default, and now the talk is only getting bigger. So let's talk about that. While a default in the US is technically possible, we believe it is unlikely. This scenario has too many negative consequences for the US themselves, and they will not resort to it as long as there are other options. US expenses consistently exceed their revenues, so they are borrowing money through government bonds, or treasuries. They are considered the most liquid and reliable assets in the world. Yet now it is becoming increasingly difficult to do so. Countries around the world refuse to buy new treasuries for various reasons, causing their price to plummet, and service costs to surge. To solve this, the US needs to get investors to buy the government bonds again. And this can be done... at the expense of another country. When the situation in some economy reaches a critical point, private investors, banks, and governments realize that it is necessary to flee from such troubled assets. And when they do, billions of dollars of freed capital are flowing to the most liquid and reliable asset in the world, the US treasuries. Earlier in the comment section to the post How the US undermining Europe, we were asked why the US is doing this. Undermining, that is. Ukraine invested half of its aid in US government debt
Ukraine has increased its investments in US government bonds 1.8 times since last February, investing an amount equal to about half of the aid received from the US authorities. According to the US Treasury Department, from February last year to the end of September this year Ukraine has increased its investments in US government debt by $10.7 billion up to $24.5 billion, which is a record level at least since 2011. At the end of last winter, the portfolio was just $13.8 billion, and at its lowest level, in the middle of last summer, it was $7.9 billion. At the same time, from February 2022 to the beginning of October this year, Ukraine received from the US financial assistance for $22.9 billion. Thus, Ukraine's investments in US government securities increased by an amount equal to half of the aid received from the US. US money coming back to the US... and why aren't we surprised?
